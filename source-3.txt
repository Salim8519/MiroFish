## 5) The most suitable business model 🔧

### The model I recommend: **B2B2C, not just B2C**

Instead of saying, “I am only an app for users,” it is better to be:

* **For the user:** discovery + comparison + booking + payment + team invitation
* **For the venue owner:** an operations dashboard + calendar + availability management + deposits + notifications + reports

Because the real problem is not only that the player “cannot find a pitch,” but also that the venue owner usually wants to:

* reduce calls and WhatsApp messages
* reduce no-shows
* fill empty hours
* sell weaker time slots
* bring in new customers

### Recommended pricing

The best starting structure, in my opinion, is:

* **8% to 12% commission** on paid bookings
* Or a **simple venue subscription** + a lower commission
* Or a **hybrid plan**: 20–30 OMR monthly + 6% to 10% commission

This structure is better than relying only on commission, because commission alone in a single city may be slow in the beginning.

I see it succeeding if you focus on these factors:

### 1) Do not start too wide

Start with **only two categories**:
**football/turf + padel**

### 2) Build a “group leader” feature

In the Gulf in general, and logically in Muscat as well, many bookings are led by one person who gathers the team.
The golden feature here is not just booking, but:

* replacing a player who drops out

This could be **your strongest winning feature** over individual venue apps.

### 4) Arabic + English from day one

This is not a “nice to have.” It is a core requirement in Muscat.

### 5) Do not become a custom app builder for each venue

That is a trap.
Your real advantage is **aggregation + operating system + demand generation**, not becoming only a white-label company.

## 8) Where could the project fail? ⚠️

It will most likely fail if one of these mistakes happens:

### 1) You start with every category at once

Pitches + gym + gaming + academies + events = early operational chaos.

### 2) You focus on the app before supply

If you do not have **10–15 respectable venues** from the beginning with real availability and real payment enabled, the app will not survive.

### 3) You do not solve double-booking

Any booking conflict in the first month can destroy trust quickly.

### 4) You do not solve no-shows

Without deposits and a clear cancellation policy, venues will get tired and start rejecting your platform.

### 5) You rely only on consumer-side demand

Competition here is not just from other apps, but also from:

* direct calls
* WhatsApp
* Instagram
* each club’s own app
* and regional platforms such as Malaeb ([malaebapp.com][2])

### 6) You ignore regulatory requirements

If you will sell online and collect payments, you need to handle licensing and commercial verification properly through the government platforms mentioned earlier. ([Oman Observer][3])
